About Our Society

◆

The Qur'aan and Sunnah Society of Seychelles is a community organisation dedicated to the propagation of authentic Islamic knowledge based purely upon the Book of Allah and the authentic Sunnah of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).

We operate and manage our local Masjid facilities in Mont Fleuri, offering daily congregational prayers, authentic knowledge structures, seminars, and charity distributions for brothers and sisters across Mahe.

Our History

◆
2000
Registration

A Society Is Founded

The Qur'aan and Sunnah Society of Seychelles was officially registered on the 10th October 2000, with a common goal of establishing an Islamic Centre comprising a masjid, classrooms, an information centre, a library, and an office.

Islam was expanding among the Seychellois in the mid-nineties, and there was a growing need to decentralise the Islamic activities then concentrated at the single masjid in Victoria — the only masjid in Seychelles at that time.

Early Days
La Louise

Humble Beginnings

Due to limited funds and facilities, the Society initially functioned from La Louise — the residence of Imam Idris Yusuf, the first Seychellois imam to graduate from the Islamic University of Madinah, Saudi Arabia.

Daily activities included the five obligatory salah, Islamic knowledge sessions grounded in authentic 'aqeedah, propagation of Islam, and administrative work. Jumu'ah was performed under a large mango tree beneath a tent rented each week from SIDEC.

"Each week the few Muslim brothers willingly contributed funds to cater for the rental of the tent."
Growth
Beaufond Lane · Mont Fleuri

A Permanent Home Takes Shape

As the local Muslim population grew, membership fees allowed the Society to rent a carpentry workshop from Mr Fernand Laporte at Beaufond Lane, Mont Fleuri. All activities were relocated to this new address — the very land the Masjid stands on today.

Brothers contributed financially and physically to convert the workshop into a prayer hall, separated by curtains to accommodate male and female worshippers, with a small office and library alongside.

Expansion
Education & Outreach

Knowledge Programmes Flourish

As the centre gained recognition, its programmes intensified. Islamic lessons in 'aqeedah, fiqh, and hadeeth were conducted for both men and women, alongside a madrassa for children. Weekend lectures and full-day educational programmes became regular features.

During Ramadan, sponsored iftar and dinner programmes were organised. For da'wah, adverts were placed in the local newspaper twice monthly offering free distribution of books, and visitors were welcomed to the centre.

2007
Land Acquisition

The Land Is Secured

The centre had begun attracting foreign Muslim visitors, and contacts were established to seek sponsors for the construction of a proper masjid. Alhamdulillah, in 2007 Mr Fernand Laporte agreed to sell the land housing the centre to the Society.

An adjacent plot belonging to Mr Decise Hoarau was also purchased, made possible through the generous sponsorship of DAR AL BER Society — a charitable organisation based in Dubai.

"Upon his visit to the centre, a member of the UAE royal family expressed his desire to sponsor the masjid project — alhamdulillah."
2013
Completion

The Masjid Is Complete

The masjid project was completed in the year 2013, alhamdulillah. The Qur'aan and Sunnah Society now operates from a fully equipped facility: a spacious masjid, four classrooms, an office, a janaza room, a library, and an information centre — His Highness Sheikh Dr. Sultan Bin Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan's Masjid at Beaufond Lane, Mont Fleuri, Mahé, Seychelles.
